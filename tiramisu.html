Tiramisu Recipe

Description

Rich, creamy, and delicately sweet, this classic Italian tiramisu is the perfect balance of bold espresso, velvety mascarpone, and a hint of liqueur. Layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and luscious mascarpone cream create a dessert that's both elegant and comforting. This no-bake treat is perfect for special occasions or whenever you crave something indulgent. Follow this traditional recipe for a taste of Italy that will impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth.

Ingredients

  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1 pound (16 oz) mascarpone cheese, room temperature
  • 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 3/4 cups strong espresso or coffee, room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons coffee liqueur (like Kahlúa) or dark rum
  • 2 packages (7 oz each) Italian ladyfinger cookies (about 24-28 cookies)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings for garnish (optional)
  • Fresh berries for serving (optional)

    • Steps

    1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar in a heatproof bowl until pale yellow.
    2. Place bowl over simmering water and continue whisking until mixture reaches 160°F.
    3. Remove from heat and let cool for 10 minutes.
    4. Beat mascarpone into the egg mixture until smooth.
    5. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream and vanilla to stiff peaks.
    6. Gently fold whipped cream into mascarpone mixture.
    7. Combine espresso and coffee liqueur in a shallow dish.
    8. Quickly dip ladyfingers into coffee mixture (about 1 second per side).
    9. Arrange soaked ladyfingers in a single layer in a 9×13 inch dish.
    10. Spread half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
    11. Add another layer of soaked ladyfingers.
    12. Top with remaining mascarpone mixture, spreading evenly.
    13. Cover and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.
    14. Cut into squares and serve chilled, with berries if using.
